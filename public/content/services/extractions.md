# Tooth Extractions

While we always strive to save natural teeth, sometimes extraction is the best option for your oral health. We provide gentle, comfortable tooth removal procedures with a focus on patient comfort and proper healing.

## When Extraction is Necessary

- **Severe Decay**: Tooth damage beyond repair
- **Advanced Gum Disease**: Loosened teeth from bone loss
- **Impacted Wisdom Teeth**: Causing pain or crowding
- **Orthodontic Treatment**: Creating space for proper alignment
- **Infection**: When root canal is not viable
- **Broken Teeth**: Severe fractures below the gum line
- **Overcrowding**: To prepare for braces or dentures
- **Baby Teeth**: That haven't fallen out properly

## Types of Extractions

### Simple Extraction
For visible teeth above the gum line. Quick procedure with local anesthesia performed in our office.

**Process:**
- Local anesthesia applied
- Tooth loosened with elevator
- Tooth removed with forceps
- Gauze applied to control bleeding
- Usually 20-30 minutes

### Surgical Extraction
For teeth that haven't erupted or are broken below the gum line. May require sedation.

**Process:**
- Anesthesia and/or sedation
- Small incision in gum tissue
- Tooth sectioned if needed
- Careful removal of tooth
- Stitches to close incision
- Typically 45-90 minutes

### Wisdom Teeth Removal
Specialized extraction of third molars that are impacted or problematic.

**Common Reasons:**
- Impaction causing pain
- Infection or cysts
- Damage to adjacent teeth
- Crowding issues
- Preventive removal

## Before & After Care Instructions

### Before Your Appointment
- Eat a good meal (you'll be numb afterward)
- Take prescribed antibiotics if provided
- Arrange transportation if receiving sedation
- Wear comfortable clothing
- Avoid smoking for 24 hours before

### Immediately After Extraction (First 24 Hours)

**Do:**
- Bite firmly on gauze for 30-45 minutes
- Apply ice packs (20 min on, 20 min off)
- Take pain medication as prescribed
- Rest and avoid strenuous activity
- Eat soft, cool foods (yogurt, ice cream, smoothies)
- Keep head elevated when lying down

**Don't:**
- Disturb the blood clot
- Rinse vigorously or spit
- Drink through a straw
- Smoke or use tobacco
- Drink alcohol
- Consume hot beverages

### Days 2-7: Healing Phase

**Do:**
- Gentle saltwater rinses (after 24 hours)
- Continue soft food diet
- Gradually return to normal activities
- Maintain oral hygiene (avoid extraction site)
- Take all prescribed medications
- Apply warm compresses for swelling

**Don't:**
- Smoke (delays healing)
- Vigorous exercise for 3-5 days
- Eat hard, crunchy, or sticky foods
- Touch extraction site with tongue or fingers

### Long-Term Care
- Follow up with replacement options
- Attend scheduled check-ups
- Maintain excellent oral hygiene
- Consider dental implant or bridge

## Tooth Replacement Options

After extraction, we discuss replacement options to restore function and appearance:

### Dental Implants
- Most permanent solution
- Preserves bone structure
- Functions like natural tooth
- Lasts a lifetime

### Dental Bridges
- Fixed replacement option
- Anchors to adjacent teeth
- Natural appearance
- Lasts 10-15 years

### Partial Dentures
- Removable option
- Cost-effective
- Replaces multiple teeth
- Can be upgraded later

### Full Dentures
- For multiple extractions
- Restores full smile
- Custom-fitted
- Improves eating and speaking

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will tooth extraction hurt?**  
A: The procedure itself is painless due to anesthesia. Some discomfort during healing is normal and manageable with medication.

**Q: How long does extraction take?**  
A: Simple extractions take 20-30 minutes. Surgical extractions may take 45-90 minutes.

**Q: When can I eat normally?**  
A: Stick to soft foods for 3-5 days, then gradually return to normal diet as comfort allows.

**Q: What is dry socket and how do I prevent it?**  
A: Dry socket occurs when the blood clot dislodges. Prevent it by avoiding straws, smoking, and vigorous rinsing for 72 hours.

**Q: How long does it take to heal?**  
A: Initial healing: 1-2 weeks. Complete bone healing: 3-6 months.

**Q: Can I work the next day?**  
A: Most patients can return to work within 1-2 days. Complex extractions may require more time.

**Q: Will I need stitches?**  
A: Simple extractions usually don't require stitches. Surgical extractions often do, which dissolve on their own.

**Q: What if I have a dental emergency after hours?**  
A: Contact our emergency line. We're here to help with any complications.

**Q: Should I replace an extracted tooth?**  
A: Yes, to prevent bone loss, teeth shifting, and bite problems. We'll discuss the best option for you.

## Warning Signs: When to Call Us

Contact us immediately if you experience:
- Bleeding that won't stop after 2 hours
- Severe pain not relieved by medication
- Fever over 101°F
- Excessive swelling or difficulty swallowing
- Bad taste or odor from extraction site
- Numbness lasting more than 8 hours
- Allergic reaction to medications

We're committed to making your extraction experience as comfortable as possible while protecting your long-term oral health.
