# Custom Nightguards

A nightguard is a custom-fitted dental appliance worn during sleep to protect your teeth from the damaging effects of bruxism (teeth grinding and clenching). Our custom nightguards provide superior comfort and protection compared to over-the-counter options.

## Signs You May Need a Nightguard

- Waking up with jaw pain or headaches
- Tooth sensitivity or worn tooth surfaces
- Tight or tired jaw muscles
- Partner notices grinding sounds during sleep
- Chipped, cracked, or flattened teeth
- Indentations on tongue or cheek tissue
- Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) discomfort

## Benefits & Advantages

### Tooth Protection
- Prevents tooth wear and enamel erosion
- Reduces risk of chips and fractures
- Protects existing dental work (crowns, veneers, fillings)
- Preserves tooth structure

### Jaw & Muscle Relief
- Reduces jaw muscle tension
- Alleviates morning headaches
- Decreases facial pain
- Minimizes TMJ disorder symptoms
- Improves sleep quality

### Long-Term Benefits
- Prevents costly dental repairs
- Maintains proper bite alignment
- Reduces neck and shoulder tension
- Custom fit ensures comfort throughout the night
- Durable construction lasts years with proper care

## What to Expect During Treatment

### Initial Consultation
1. **Comprehensive Examination**: Evaluation of teeth, jaw, and bite
2. **Diagnosis**: Assessment of grinding/clenching severity
3. **Discussion**: Review of symptoms and treatment goals
4. **Treatment Plan**: Recommendation of nightguard type

### Impressions Appointment
1. **Precision Molds**: Detailed impressions of upper and lower teeth
2. **Bite Registration**: Recording of your natural bite position
3. **Material Selection**: Choice of nightguard material and thickness
4. **Fabrication**: Custom creation in dental laboratory (1-2 weeks)

### Delivery Appointment
1. **Fitting**: Trying in custom nightguard
2. **Adjustments**: Fine-tuning for perfect fit
3. **Instructions**: Proper insertion, removal, and care
4. **Comfort Check**: Ensuring no pressure points
5. **Follow-up**: Scheduling check appointment in 2-4 weeks

## Types of Nightguards We Offer

### Soft Nightguards
- Cushioned, comfortable material
- Best for mild to moderate grinding
- Most comfortable option
- Ideal for first-time users

### Dual-Laminate Nightguards
- Soft interior, hard exterior
- Excellent for moderate to heavy grinding
- Balanced comfort and durability
- Most popular choice

### Hard Acrylic Nightguards
- Rigid, durable material
- Best for severe grinding/clenching
- Longest-lasting option
- Provides maximum protection

## Before & After Care Instructions

### When You First Receive Your Nightguard
- Wear every night, even if symptoms improve
- Expect 3-7 days adjustment period
- May produce extra saliva initially (normal)
- Practice inserting and removing before bed
- Bring to follow-up appointments for adjustments

### Daily Care Routine

**Every Morning:**
- Remove and rinse with cool water
- Brush gently with soft toothbrush
- Use mild soap or denture cleaner (not toothpaste)
- Rinse thoroughly and air dry
- Store in ventilated case

**Weekly Deep Clean:**
- Soak in denture cleaning solution
- Or use 1:1 white vinegar and water solution (15-20 minutes)
- Brush thoroughly and rinse well
- Check for wear or damage

### Storage Guidelines
- Always use provided case
- Store in cool, dry place
- Keep away from pets (they're attracted to the scent)
- Avoid hot water or direct sunlight
- Never wrap in tissue or napkin

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will a nightguard stop me from grinding?**  
A: Nightguards don't stop grinding but protect your teeth from damage and reduce muscle strain.

**Q: How long does it take to get used to wearing one?**  
A: Most patients adjust within 3-7 nights. Persistence is key!

**Q: Can I wear my nightguard during the day?**  
A: While designed for nighttime, it can be worn during stressful daytime situations if needed.

**Q: Will my insurance cover a nightguard?**  
A: Many dental plans cover nightguards partially or fully. We'll verify your benefits.

**Q: How is a custom nightguard different from store-bought?**  
A: Custom guards fit precisely, are more comfortable, more durable, and provide better protection.

**Q: How do I know when to replace it?**  
A: Signs include visible wear, holes, cracks, poor fit, or discomfort. Typically lasts 2-5 years.
