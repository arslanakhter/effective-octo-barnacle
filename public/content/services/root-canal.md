# Root Canal Treatment

Root canal therapy is a dental procedure that treats infection at the center of a tooth. When the pulp becomes infected due to decay or injury, root canal treatment can save the tooth and prevent the infection from spreading.

## Why You Might Need a Root Canal

- Deep tooth decay reaching the pulp
- Cracked or fractured teeth
- Repeated dental procedures on the same tooth
- Severe tooth pain or sensitivity
- Swelling or tenderness in nearby gums
- Darkening of the tooth

## Benefits & Advantages

- **Pain Relief**: Eliminates the source of severe tooth pain
- **Tooth Preservation**: Saves your natural tooth instead of extraction
- **Prevents Infection Spread**: Stops bacteria from affecting surrounding teeth
- **Maintains Natural Function**: Preserves your natural bite and chewing ability
- **Cost-Effective**: Less expensive than tooth replacement options
- **High Success Rate**: Over 95% success rate when properly maintained

## What to Expect During Treatment

### Before the Procedure
- Comprehensive examination and X-rays
- Discussion of sedation options if needed
- Review of treatment plan and timeline

### During the Procedure
1. **Anesthesia Administration**: Local anesthesia ensures complete comfort
2. **Access Opening**: Small opening created in the tooth crown
3. **Pulp Removal**: Infected or damaged pulp tissue carefully removed
4. **Canal Cleaning**: Thorough cleaning and shaping of root canals
5. **Filling & Sealing**: Canals filled with biocompatible material
6. **Temporary Filling**: Tooth sealed with temporary filling

### After the Procedure
- Most patients return to normal activities immediately
- Mild sensitivity for a few days is normal
- Crown placement scheduled within 2-3 weeks

## Before & After Care Instructions

### Before Your Appointment
- Take any prescribed antibiotics as directed
- Eat a meal beforehand (you may be numb for hours after)
- Avoid alcohol 24 hours before treatment
- Arrange transportation if receiving sedation

### After Your Appointment
- Avoid chewing on treated tooth until permanent restoration
- Take pain medication as prescribed
- Maintain regular brushing and flossing
- Apply ice packs to reduce swelling (20 minutes on, 20 off)
- Avoid hard, sticky, or crunchy foods for 24-48 hours
- Attend follow-up appointments for crown placement

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is a root canal painful?**  
A: Modern root canal therapy is virtually painless. Local anesthesia ensures comfort throughout the procedure. Most patients report the experience is similar to getting a filling.

**Q: How long does a root canal take?**  
A: Most root canals can be completed in 60-90 minutes. Complex cases may require a second visit.

**Q: Will I need a crown after root canal treatment?**  
A: Yes, in most cases. A crown protects the treated tooth and restores its full function and appearance.

**Q: How long will my root canal last?**  
A: With proper care and a crown, root canal-treated teeth can last a lifetime.

**Q: What are the alternatives to root canal treatment?**  
A: The only alternative is tooth extraction, followed by replacement with an implant, bridge, or partial denture.

**Q: Can all teeth be treated with root canal therapy?**  
A: Most teeth can be saved with root canal treatment. Our dentist will evaluate your specific case and discuss the best options.
