# Composite Fillings

Composite fillings are tooth-colored restorations made of a durable plastic and glass mixture. They blend seamlessly with your natural teeth while effectively treating cavities and minor tooth damage. Unlike traditional silver amalgam fillings, composite fillings are virtually invisible and bond directly to tooth structure.

## Why Choose Composite Fillings

### Aesthetic Benefits
- Match your natural tooth color perfectly
- Virtually invisible in your smile
- No dark or metallic appearance
- Ideal for front and back teeth
- Color-stable over time

### Functional Advantages
- Bond directly to tooth structure
- Require less tooth removal than amalgam
- Strengthen remaining tooth structure
- Seal completely against bacteria
- Minimize sensitivity to temperature
- Versatile for various repairs

## When You Need a Composite Filling

- Cavities and tooth decay
- Chipped or broken teeth
- Worn tooth edges
- Small fractures
- Gaps between teeth
- Replacing old amalgam fillings
- Cosmetic improvements to tooth shape

## Benefits & Advantages

### Health & Safety
- Mercury-free alternative
- Biocompatible materials
- No risk of metal allergies
- BPA-free options available
- Safe for all ages

### Durability & Performance
- Last 7-10 years or longer
- Resist fracture in small to mid-sized fillings
- Can be repaired if damaged
- Minimal expansion and contraction
- Less likely to crack remaining tooth

### Convenience
- Can be completed in single visit
- Quick procedure (30-60 minutes)
- Immediate use after placement
- Minimal post-treatment sensitivity
- Sets quickly with curing light

## What to Expect During Treatment

### Diagnosis & Planning
1. **Examination**: Visual and X-ray assessment
2. **Cavity Detection**: Precise identification of decay
3. **Treatment Plan**: Discussion of procedure and options
4. **Shade Matching**: Selection of perfect color match

### The Procedure

**Step 1: Preparation**
- Local anesthesia for comfort (if needed)
- Isolation of treatment area
- Complete numbing verified

**Step 2: Decay Removal**
- Careful removal of all decay
- Preservation of healthy tooth structure
- Cleaning and disinfection of cavity

**Step 3: Tooth Preparation**
- Application of bonding agent
- Etching to create strong bond
- Preparation for composite material

**Step 4: Filling Placement**
- Layer-by-layer application
- Custom shaping to match tooth anatomy
- Light curing to harden each layer
- Precise sculpting of chewing surface

**Step 5: Finishing**
- Bite adjustment for comfort
- Polishing to smooth finish
- Final inspection
- Post-treatment instructions

## Before & After Care Instructions

### Before Your Appointment
- Brush and floss normally
- Eat a meal beforehand
- List any medication allergies
- Arrive a few minutes early

### Immediately After Treatment
- Wait until numbness wears off before eating
- Avoid hot beverages while numb
- No hard or sticky foods for 24 hours
- Gentle brushing around treated area
- Mild sensitivity is normal for 1-2 days

### First 24-48 Hours
- Chew on opposite side if possible
- Avoid extremely hot or cold foods
- No hard candy or ice chewing
- Take over-the-counter pain relief if needed
- Avoid staining foods/drinks (coffee, red wine)

### Long-Term Care
- Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
- Floss daily around all fillings
- Regular dental checkups and cleanings
- Avoid chewing ice or hard objects
- Don't use teeth as tools
- Consider fluoride treatments for cavity prevention

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do composite fillings hurt?**  
A: The procedure is performed with local anesthesia, so you shouldn't feel pain. Mild sensitivity afterward is normal and temporary.

**Q: How long do composite fillings last?**  
A: With proper care, composite fillings typically last 7-10 years, and often longer.

**Q: Are composite fillings as strong as silver fillings?**  
A: For small to medium cavities, yes. Very large cavities may require alternative treatments like crowns.

**Q: Will my insurance cover composite fillings?**  
A: Most insurance plans cover composite fillings, at least for front teeth. We'll verify your coverage.

**Q: Can composite fillings stain?**  
A: High-quality composites are stain-resistant, but excessive coffee, tea, or tobacco can cause slight discoloration over time.

**Q: Can old amalgam fillings be replaced with composite?**  
A: Yes! We can safely remove amalgam fillings and replace them with tooth-colored composite.

**Q: How soon can I eat after a composite filling?**  
A: Since composite hardens immediately, you can eat as soon as the numbing wears off.

**Q: Will I be able to see the filling?**  
A: No! We carefully match the composite to your tooth color for a seamless, natural appearance.
