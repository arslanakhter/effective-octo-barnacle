# Dental Exam and Cleaning

Regular dental exams and professional cleanings are the foundation of excellent oral health. These preventive appointments help detect problems early and keep your teeth and gums in optimal condition.

## What's Included in Your Exam

- **Comprehensive Oral Examination**: Thorough assessment of teeth, gums, and oral tissues
- **Digital X-Rays**: When needed to detect hidden problems
- **Oral Cancer Screening**: Early detection saves lives
- **Gum Disease Assessment**: Measuring pocket depths and checking for inflammation
- **Bite and Jaw Evaluation**: Identifying alignment or TMJ issues
- **Existing Restoration Check**: Monitoring fillings, crowns, and other work

## Professional Cleaning Services

### Routine Prophylaxis
Standard cleaning for healthy mouths with minimal buildup.

### Deep Cleaning (Scaling & Root Planing)
For patients with gum disease or heavy tartar accumulation.

### Periodontal Maintenance
Ongoing care after gum disease treatment.

## Benefits of Regular Visits

- **Early Problem Detection**: Catch cavities and gum disease early
- **Prevent Tooth Loss**: Maintain healthy gums and bone
- **Fresher Breath**: Remove bacteria causing bad breath
- **Brighter Smile**: Remove surface stains and polish teeth
- **Overall Health**: Oral health connected to heart disease, diabetes, and more
- **Cost Savings**: Prevention costs less than treatment

## How Often Should You Visit?

Most patients benefit from cleanings every 6 months. However, you may need more frequent visits if you have:

- History of gum disease
- Dry mouth or reduced saliva
- Current tobacco use
- Diabetes or heart disease
- Tendency to develop cavities or tartar

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does teeth cleaning hurt?**  
A: Most cleanings are painless. If you have sensitivity, we can use numbing gel or anesthesia.

**Q: How long does an appointment take?**  
A: Typically 45-60 minutes for exam and cleaning combined.

**Q: Will my insurance cover it?**  
A: Most dental insurance plans cover preventive visits 100% twice per year.

**Q: Do I really need X-rays every visit?**  
A: No, typically X-rays are taken once per year or as needed to diagnose specific concerns.
