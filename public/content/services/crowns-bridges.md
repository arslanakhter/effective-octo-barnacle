# Dental Crowns and Bridges

Dental crowns and bridges are custom-made restorations designed to repair damaged teeth or replace missing ones. These solutions restore both function and aesthetics to your smile with natural-looking, durable results.

## Dental Crowns

Crowns are tooth-shaped caps that cover damaged or weakened teeth, providing strength, protection, and improved appearance.

### When You Need a Crown

- After root canal treatment
- To repair broken, cracked, or fractured teeth
- To strengthen teeth with large fillings
- To improve appearance of severely discolored teeth
- To reshape misshapen teeth
- To protect dental implants
- To restore severely worn teeth

## Dental Bridges

Bridges replace one or more missing teeth by anchoring to adjacent natural teeth or implants, literally "bridging" the gap.

### Types of Bridges

- **Traditional Bridges**: Most common, anchored to natural teeth on both sides
- **Cantilever Bridges**: Supported on one side only, used when teeth are present on only one side
- **Maryland Bridges**: Metal or porcelain framework bonded to back of adjacent teeth
- **Implant-Supported Bridges**: Most stable option, doesn't impact adjacent teeth

## Benefits & Advantages

### Crowns
- Restore tooth strength and function
- Protect weakened teeth from further damage
- Improve tooth appearance and smile aesthetics
- Last 10-15 years or longer with proper care
- Custom-made to match your natural teeth
- Biocompatible materials safe for oral use

### Bridges
- Fill gaps and restore your smile
- Maintain face shape and prevent bone loss
- Restore ability to chew and speak properly
- Distribute bite forces evenly
- Prevent remaining teeth from shifting
- Fixed solution that doesn't require removal

## What to Expect During Treatment

### First Appointment (Preparation)
1. **Examination**: Comprehensive evaluation and X-rays
2. **Preparation**: Tooth reshaped to accommodate crown/bridge
3. **Impressions**: Precise molds taken for custom fabrication
4. **Temporary**: Temporary restoration placed for protection
5. **Shade Selection**: Perfect color match chosen

### Between Appointments
- Temporary restoration protects prepared tooth
- Custom crown/bridge crafted in dental laboratory
- Typically 2-3 weeks for fabrication

### Second Appointment (Placement)
1. **Temporary Removal**: Temporary restoration carefully removed
2. **Trial Fit**: New restoration checked for fit and color
3. **Adjustments**: Minor modifications if needed
4. **Cementation**: Permanent bonding with dental cement
5. **Bite Check**: Ensuring proper alignment and comfort

## Before & After Care Instructions

### Before Your Appointment
- Brush and floss normally
- List any medications you're taking
- Mention any dental sensitivity
- Eat before appointment (you'll be numb after)

### After Preparation Visit
- Avoid sticky or hard foods with temporary
- Chew on opposite side of mouth
- Brush gently around temporary crown/bridge
- Contact us if temporary becomes loose
- Avoid extremely hot or cold foods

### After Final Placement
- Sensitivity may last a few days
- Avoid very hard foods for 24 hours
- Resume normal oral hygiene
- Floss daily (especially around bridges)
- Regular dental checkups every 6 months

## Long-Term Care

- Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
- Floss daily, using floss threaders for bridges
- Avoid chewing ice or hard objects
- Don't use teeth as tools
- Wear nightguard if you grind teeth
- Attend regular dental cleanings
- Avoid staining foods/drinks in first 48 hours

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long do crowns and bridges last?**  
A: With proper care, crowns and bridges typically last 10-15 years or longer. Some patients have restorations that last 25+ years.

**Q: Will my crown look natural?**  
A: Absolutely! Modern materials and techniques create crowns virtually indistinguishable from natural teeth.

**Q: Is the procedure painful?**  
A: The procedure is performed under local anesthesia. Most patients report minimal discomfort afterward.

**Q: Can I eat normally with a crown or bridge?**  
A: Yes! After a brief adjustment period, you can eat all your favorite foods normally.

**Q: What if my crown falls off?**  
A: Contact us immediately. Keep the crown safe and avoid chewing on that tooth. We can often re-cement it.

**Q: Do crowns require special care?**  
A: No special care needed beyond regular brushing, flossing, and dental visits.

**Q: How much do crowns and bridges cost?**  
A: Costs vary based on material and complexity. We'll provide a detailed estimate and discuss insurance coverage.

**Q: Can a crowned tooth still get cavities?**  
A: The crown itself cannot decay, but the tooth structure underneath can. Proper oral hygiene is essential.
